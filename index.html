Navigate IFRS and US GAAP requirements with confidence as AI transforms your business.

Whether you're fine-tuning foundation models, implementing RAG systems, or investing in training data—understanding when to capitalize versus expense has never been more critical.

The Challenge

Why AI Accounting Is Complex

Traditional software accounting frameworks weren't designed for iterative, data-dependent AI development.

⚡

Blurred Phase Boundaries

Research and development activities often overlap in iterative ML workflows.

⏱️

Rapid Obsolescence

AI models can become outdated within months, challenging useful life assumptions.

🧩

Novel Cost Components

Training data, compute, and model weights don't map to traditional software cost categories in IAS 38 or ASC 350-40.

📐

Limited Explicit Guidance

Standards don't address whether fine-tuning creates a new asset, how to treat adapter weights, or when RAG is capitalizable.

Decision Framework

Capitalize or Expense?

Capitalization criteria for internally developed AI under IFRS and US GAAP.

IAS 38.57

IFRS Approach

  • → Research phase EXPENSE (no retrospective capitalization)
  • → Development phase CAPITALIZE prospectively when ALL 6 criteria met:
  • 1 Technical feasibility to complete the asset
  • 2 Intention to complete and use or sell
  • 3 Ability to use or sell the asset
  • 4 Probable future economic benefits demonstrable
  • 5 Adequate resources (technical, financial, other) available
  • 6 Expenditure reliably measurable during development
ASC 350-40

US GAAP Approach (Internal-Use Software)

  • → Preliminary stage EXPENSE (conceptual formulation, vendor evaluation)
  • → Application development CAPITALIZE (management authorizes & commits funding)
  • → Post-implementation EXPENSE (training, maintenance)
  • • Upgrades/enhancements → capitalize only if additional functionality
  • • Training and data conversion → always expense
  • • Cloud/hosting (service contract): may capitalize implementation costs (ASU 2018-15)

Focus Area

Training Data Acquisition

Data is often the most significant—and complex—cost component in AI development.

🛒

Third-Party Data Licensing

Purchased datasets with usage rights. Consider whether the license is perpetual or term-based, exclusive or non-exclusive.

May qualify as separate intangible
🏷️

Data Labeling & Annotation

Human or automated labeling to create supervised learning datasets. Directly attributable to model development.

May capitalize if post-feasibility
🧹

Data Cleaning & Preparation

ETL processes, deduplication, normalization, and quality assurance to make data model-ready.

Consider allocation to phases
🔮

Synthetic Data Generation

Creating artificial training data using simulation, augmentation, or generative models.

Emerging area—document rationale
📚

Domain Corpora for CPT

Specialized text collections (legal, medical, financial) for continued pre-training of foundation models.

May qualify as separate asset
🔐

Proprietary Internal Data

Using existing enterprise data for training. Consider incremental costs vs. already-capitalized systems.

Assess incremental costs only

Key Judgment: Separate Asset or Model Component?

Reusable datasets that can train multiple models may warrant recognition as separate intangible assets with their own useful life assessment.

Common Scenario

Building on Foundation Models

Most enterprises don't train models from scratch—they adapt existing ones.

01

Continued Pre-Training (CPT)

Extending a foundation model's training on domain-specific data before task-specific fine-tuning.

  • Costs: Domain corpora acquisition, compute for additional training epochs
  • Key question: Does CPT create a distinct asset or enhance the base license?
  • Consideration: Document the technical and economic separation from base model
02

Fine-Tuning & Adapters

Task-specific adaptation using supervised learning, RLHF, or parameter-efficient methods (LoRA).

  • Costs: Labeled datasets, compute, evaluation infrastructure
  • Key question: When does experimentation end and development begin?
  • Consideration: Adapter weights may be separable from base model costs
03

RAG Implementation

Combining LLMs with retrieval systems, vector databases, and enterprise knowledge bases.

  • Costs: Embedding models, vector DB infrastructure, chunking pipelines
  • Key question: Is the retrieval layer a separate asset or integrated system?
  • Consideration: Knowledge base may have different useful life than LLM layer
04

Prompt Engineering & Orchestration

System prompts, chains, agents, and workflow orchestration built around foundation model APIs.

  • Costs: Development labor, testing infrastructure, API consumption
  • Key question: Does this create capitalizable software or operational process?
  • Consideration: Thin layer over SaaS may not meet recognition criteria

Subsequent Measurement

Amortization & Useful Life

Critical considerations for AI asset measurement after recognition.

📅 Useful Life Uncertainty

No established norms exist yet. Entities must apply significant judgment considering technology cycles, competitive dynamics, and model performance decay.

📉 Model Drift Factor

Performance degradation over time may indicate impairment or shortened useful life requiring reassessment.

🔄 Continuous Learning

Self-improving models challenge the boundary between maintenance and enhancement—document the distinction.

⚠️ Impairment Triggers

New competitor models, regulatory changes, or data quality issues may signal impairment testing need.

Self-Assessment

Questions to Ask

Key considerations for your AI accounting policies.

1

Classification & Timing

  • What evidence documents the shift from research/preliminary to development/application stage?
  • How do we demonstrate all 6 IAS 38 criteria (IFRS) or project stage entry (US GAAP)?
  • How do we handle iterative ML workflows where phases overlap?
2

Data & Foundation Models

  • What is the unit of account—individual model, model version, or integrated system?
  • Do training datasets meet separate recognition criteria as distinct intangibles?
  • Does CPT/fine-tuning create a new asset or enhance the underlying license?
3

Cost Identification

  • Do employee and contractor costs meet "directly attributable" criteria (IAS 38.66)?
  • How do we allocate shared compute between capitalizable and non-capitalizable activities?
  • Are overhead and borrowing costs included or excluded during development?
4

Ongoing Assessment

  • Does retraining add future benefits (capitalize) or maintain current function (expense)?
  • What indicators trigger impairment review (model drift, new competitors, regulatory)?
  • How do we document useful life estimates and reassess at each reporting date?
